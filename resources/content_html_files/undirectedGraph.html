Undirected Graph

An undirected graph is a graph in which edges do not have any direction. That is, the nodes are unordered pairs in the definition of every edge.

Characteristics of an Undirected Graph

  • Edges in an undirected graph are bidirectional in nature.
  • In an undirected graph, there is no concept of a “parent” or “child” vertex as there is no direction to the edges.
  • An undirected graph may contain loops, which are edges that connect a vertex to itself.
  • Degree of each vertex is the same as the total number of edges connected to it.

Applications

  • Peer-to-Peer Networks: In file-sharing or communication networks where any peer can communicate with any other without directionality.
  • Social Networks (Friendships): When friendships are mutual, they are best represented as undirected edges between nodes (individuals).
  • Collaborative Filtering in Recommendations: Represents users and items with edges indicating associations, with no directed flow needed.
